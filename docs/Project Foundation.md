# 📄 Project Foundation Document (Phase 1)

# 1. Vision Statement

The vision of this project is to build a professional, end-to-end data analytics platform that continuously analyzes publicly available technology job market data through a scalable analytics platform. Version 1 focuses on Kenya, with an architecture designed to support future expansion into additional countries and regions. By the end of July 2026, Version 1 of the platform will be fully functional, demonstrating the complete data lifecycle—from data collection and cleaning to storage, analysis, visualization, and storytelling. Throughout Q3 and beyond, the platform will continue to grow through automated data collection, richer analyses, and new insights that benefit job seekers, recruiters, hiring managers, researchers, and the wider technology community.

Beyond serving as an information resource, this project is intended to become the flagship demonstration of my technical capabilities in data analytics, data engineering, and business intelligence.

# 3. Problem Statement

Technology job markets generate significant amounts of publicly available hiring information across multiple platforms. However, this information is fragmented, inconsistent, and difficult to analyze at scale. As a result, job seekers often rely on assumptions, outdated advice, or isolated job advertisements when deciding which technical skills to develop or which opportunities to pursue.

Recruiters and hiring managers also lack easily accessible summaries of broader hiring trends, while students and aspiring professionals frequently struggle to identify which technologies, programming languages, and business intelligence tools are most valuable in today's market.

This project addresses that gap by collecting, organizing, cleaning, and analyzing publicly available job market data to answer important questions about hiring patterns, technical skill demand, experience requirements, industries, company types, and labor market trends. Rather than functioning as a job board, the platform focuses on generating meaningful insights that support informed decision-making.

---

# 4. Project Objectives

The primary objective of Version 1 is to build a complete end-to-end analytics solution that demonstrates professional data analytics and data engineering practices while generating valuable insights from real-world job market data.

To achieve this objective, the project will:

- Build an automated pipeline that collects publicly available technology job postings, beginning with Kenya in Version 1.
- Clean and standardize inconsistent and messy job market data.
- Store processed data within a well-designed PostgreSQL database.
- Develop an interactive dashboard that communicates key hiring trends and labor market insights.
- Analyze demand for technical skills, programming languages, tools, experience levels, industries, locations, and company hiring patterns.
- Apply best practices in Python programming, SQL, data modeling, ETL, documentation, and data visualization.
- Demonstrate professional software development practices including version control, project documentation, and continuous improvement.
- Produce meaningful reports and visual stories that communicate findings clearly to both technical and non-technical audiences.

# 7. Research Questions

The project seeks to answer a series of practical questions about technology employment landscape through data analysis. Version 1 investigates the Kenyan technology employment landscape while establishing a framework that can later support regional and global comparisons.

## Hiring Trends

- Which companies are hiring the most technology professionals?
- Which industries show the highest demand for technology talent?
- Which locations within Kenya have the greatest concentration of technology opportunities?
- How do hiring trends change over time?

## Technical Skills

- Which programming languages are requested most frequently?
- Which business intelligence and analytics tools are most in demand?
- Which cloud technologies appear most often?
- Which combinations of technical skills are commonly requested together?
- How do required skills differ across industries and company sizes?

## Experience Requirements

- What experience levels are requested most frequently?
- How do technical requirements differ between entry-level, mid-level, and senior positions?
- Do startups, multinational organizations, and established enterprises prioritize different skill sets?

## Salary Insights

Where salary information is available:

- Which technology roles offer the highest advertised salaries?
- How does experience influence salary expectations?
- Which industries provide the most competitive compensation?

## Work Arrangements

- What proportion of opportunities are remote, hybrid, or on-site?
- Which organizations are more likely to offer flexible working arrangements?

## Career Insights

- Which technical skills provide the greatest employment opportunities for aspiring data professionals?
- Which combinations of tools and technologies appear most frequently across job postings?
- Which sectors currently present the strongest opportunities for data analysts, data engineers, and related technology professionals?

# 8. Project Principles

The following principles will guide every technical and strategic decision made throughout the project.

**Real-world data over sample datasets.** Every analysis should be based on publicly available job market data rather than tutorial datasets.

**Insights over dashboards.** Visualizations should communicate meaningful findings rather than exist solely for presentation.

**Understanding over copying.** Every line of code, query, and architectural decision should be fully understood before being adopted.

**Automation over repetitive manual work.** Processes should be automated wherever practical to ensure scalability and consistency.

**Quality over quantity.** A smaller number of well-designed features is preferable to a large collection of incomplete functionality.

**Consistency over perfection.** Continuous progress and regular improvement are more valuable than delaying releases in pursuit of perfection.

**Every feature must answer a research question.** New functionality should only be added if it contributes to answering one or more of the project's defined research questions.
