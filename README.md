## 📌 About the Project

Tech Job Market Intelligence is an end-to-end data engineering and analytics project that collects, processes, analyzes, and visualizes publicly available technology job market data to uncover hiring trends, skill demand, labor market patterns, and career insights.

Rather than functioning as a job board, the platform focuses on transforming fragmented job postings into meaningful intelligence that helps people understand the evolving technology employment landscape.

Version 1 focuses on **Kenya 🇰🇪**, while the overall platform is designed to scale to additional countries and regions in future releases.

---

## 🎯 Why This Project Exists

Technology job postings contain valuable information about hiring demand, required technical skills, industries, experience levels, and workplace trends.

Unfortunately, this information is:

- scattered across multiple platforms
- difficult to analyze at scale
- inconsistent in structure
- quickly becomes outdated

This project aims to bridge that gap by building a complete analytics pipeline that transforms raw job data into insights that support better career decisions, hiring strategies, and labor market research.

---

# 🚀 Project Objectives

Version 1 aims to demonstrate the complete data lifecycle by building a professional analytics platform capable of:

- Collecting publicly available technology job market data
- Cleaning and standardizing messy datasets
- Designing a relational PostgreSQL database
- Building automated ETL pipelines
- Performing exploratory and statistical analysis
- Creating interactive dashboards
- Producing data-driven stories and insights
- Applying professional software engineering practices throughout development

---

# 🔍 Research Questions

The project seeks to answer questions such as:

- Which technical skills are most in demand?
- Which programming languages appear most frequently?
- Which BI and analytics tools are employers requesting?
- Which industries hire the most technology professionals?
- Which companies recruit most actively?
- Which experience levels dominate the market?
- How are hiring trends changing over time?
- What proportion of roles are remote, hybrid, or on-site?
- Which combinations of skills occur most often?
